  • Bridge The Wall is a non-residential program focused on exposing undergraduates (in penultimate and final years) from underrepresented groups in Artificial Intelligence, with a working knowledge of AI
    Particiapants are peered with Mentors with vast experience either in the Academia or Industry based on particpant's career preference.
    Participants get to work on exciting real world AI tasks from local and international clients, they also get the opportunity to hone their communications skills by interacting with clients to discuss business KPIs, etc
    BTW is a non-residential program and is fully remote, though absolutely free for particpants, it is mandatory for each one to mentor at least 5 undergraduates after graduating from the program and landing their first role or resuming in a related graduate prorgram (whichever is applicable)

  • Apply

    Application Requirements

    • You MUST be enrolled in an undergraduate degree, preferably in Computer Science or a related program (any other discipline will be considered)
    • You MUST self-identify as Black and/or of African descent
    • You MUST have proficiency in at least one programming language (Preferably Python)
    • You MUST either be in the penultimate OR final year of your program. (Expected graduation should be 2020 or 2021)
    • You MUST commit to mentor a minimum of 5 undergraduates on completing the program AND either resuming your graduate studies in a related field or landing your first job
    • Demonstrated interest in AI is preferable, either through participation in hackathons, courses, MOOCs, open source contribution etc

    If selected, you will be given a small stipend to support through the program and ensure full participation. Stipend is expected to cater for expenses such as Internet. In other cases, particpants may have the privilege to work from a nearby co-working space over the weekends
